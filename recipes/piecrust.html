Homemade Pie Crust

Description

This pie crust is my personal favorite and is using a food processor, which makes cutting the butter into the flour very simple. By the way, you can make a double batch of this, and freeze the individually wrapped dough for future pie-related adventures.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter - chilled, cut into tablespoon-size pieces
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 7 tbsp ice water
  • 1 tbsp cider vinegar

Steps

  1. Step 1

    2. Combine flour, salt, and butter in a food processor. Pulse until mixture resembles coarse crumbs, about 10 1-second pulses.

  2. Step 2

    3. Stir water and vinegar in a small bowl.

    Step 3

    Pour half the ice water and vinegar mixture into the flour and butter mixture. Pulse to combine, about 3 (1-second) pulses. Pour in remaining ice water and vinegar mixture. Pulse until mixture just starts to come together, about 8 (1-second) pulses.

  3. Step 4

    4. Turn dough out onto a wooden surface, pat into round shape and divide in half. Form each half into a disc about 5 inches wide.

  4. Step 5

    5. Wrap each disc in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes until ready to use.
